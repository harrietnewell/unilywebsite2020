What is an employee app?

An employee app (also known as an employee engagement app or employee communications app) brings communications, productivity tools, integrations, and search features to a native app for employees to use on their mobile devices. Whether used for connection and communications, administrative tasks like rota scheduling, or to bring productivity tools to those without computer access, an employee app is a valuable asset to any modern enterprise, particularly those with frontline or remote workers.

Why do companies use employee apps?

In recent years the employee app has grown from a nice-to-have into a must-have tool in the digital workplace. 2.7 billion people work away from a desk (that’s 80% of the global working population!), couple this with the rise in hybrid working, and a world in which smartphones outnumber computers 15 to 1 set the scene for a clear mobile advantage. By all accounts, an employee communications app is not a luxury, it has become essential. If you’re not offering an employee app, you’re actively making user experiences worse for your employees.

What are the benefits of an employee app?

Traditional communications channels favour office-based employees, leading to engagement gaps between business units. Enterprises with remote, hybrid, and frontline workforces will know all too well the difficulties involved in aligning and engaging these hard-to-reach audiences. An employee app will improve communicators, to IT, HR, and to employees themselves, who may miss out on important updates or a sense of connection to their organization.

In 2021, when Unily became the first employee experience platform provider to win 4 of the Top 10 Intranets of the Year awards with Nielsen Norman Group, every one of these winners featured a strong focus on their employee app and mobile experience.

Why is an employee app an essential tool for remote teams?

Through an employee app deskless employees are better able to make their voice heard, preventing knowledge silos and increasing morale among isolated employees. With deskless employees having the highest turnover and being at greater risk of disengaging, can you afford to not have an employee app?
